Commissioning Production Services Companies: The Risks and Pitfalls

Commissioning a production services company can be an essential step in ensuring the success of any project or endeavor. These companies provide a wide range of services that include everything from pre-production planning to post-production editing. However, before any contract is signed, it’s important to understand the risks and pitfalls that come with commissioning these services. In this blog, we’ll discuss some of the key factors that should be considered before signing a contract and offer some tips to help mitigate any potential risks.

Formalities First

The first rule of commissioning production services companies is to follow formalities. This means ensuring that all contracts and agreements are in writing and that all parties have a clear understanding of their obligations and responsibilities. Both parties must be on the same page concerning the specific services that will be provided, timelines, costs, and any other conditions of the contract.

Understanding the Risks and Pitfalls

The primary risk associated with commissioning production services companies is related to quality control. It’s essential to ensure that you’re working with a reputable company that has a track record of producing high-quality work. One way to mitigate this risk is to request references and examples of previous work from the production company. These samples can then be used to judge the quality of the company’s work and ensure that it meets your expectations.

Another risk associated with commissioning production services companies is related to intellectual property. It’s important to ensure that any work produced by the production company is legally owned by you or your organization. This means including specific clauses in your contract that guarantee ownership of any intellectual property produced or used during the project.

Finally, there can be a financial risk when working with production companies. Costs can quickly escalate, and projects can take longer than expected. To mitigate this risk, it’s essential to do your due diligence and ensure that you’re working with a reputable company that has a proven track record of delivering projects on budget and within the agreed timeline.

Conclusion

Commissioning production services companies can be an essential step in producing high-quality content and products. However, it’s important to understand the risks and pitfalls that come with this process. By following the right formalities, understanding the risks, and working with a reputable company that has a proven track record of delivering high-quality work, you can ensure that your project is successful and that you achieve your goals. Remember, always seek legal advice before signing any contract or agreement, and it never hurts to have an experienced attorney review and approve all contracts before they are executed by the parties involved.
